SOS - Evol 2011 : Seminar on Open Source Evolution

Leandro Doctos

Leandro Doctors is a PhD student at the Software Engineering Lab of the Institut d'Informatique, Faculty of Sciences, University of Mons, Belgium. He has been involved in the Libre Software Community of San Juan (Argentina) since 2002. In that year, he co-founded (and chaired for three years) the first Libre Software Users' Group of this Argentine Province. In early 2008, he graduated as Licenciado in Information Systems at the National University of San Juan. After a one-year internship in Lisbon, Portugal, he became a PhD student at the University of Mons (Belgium). His research topic involves the comparative analysis of the evolution of libre software distributions.

Towards the Comparative Analysis of Evolving Libre Software Distributions

Abstract of talk. Most empirical studies on the evolution and quality of libre software focus on individual software projects. Instead, we aim to empirically study, analyse and compare software distributions. We are interested in understanding how the environment that surrounds the considered systems affects their evolving quality. Therefore, we take into account different points of view from different stakeholders. Our goals are:

  • Help developers to assess, control and improve the quality of the project they work on (in this case, software distributions)
  • Advise users on quality criteria when choosing a software distribution
  • Gain a better understanding of evolving software environments. *We define a project's environment as the set of all possible artifacts, entities (communities, projects), processes (development and business models) and other aspects that might influence the system under study.
As a pilot case, we use and analyse package-related metrics for Debian and Ubuntu using data extracted from the Ultimate Debian Database (UDD). We aim to extend this study to other distributions as well. Even if the standard UDD has been our first choice as both data source and analysis tool, it has not been as effective as we first thought. We are now looking for ways to complement the UDD with other data sources in order to facilitate the automation of the data gathering and analysis process.
